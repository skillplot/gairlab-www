## About Prof. Ashutosh Bhatia

Prof. Ashutosh Bhatia is an **Associate Professor** in the Department of Computer Science & Information Systems at **BITS Pilani**, one of India's leading private research universities. With a research career spanning over a decade, he has established himself as an expert in emerging network technologies and, most recently, in **Generative AI and Agentic Intelligence**.

## Teaching & Mentorship Philosophy

Prof. Bhatia is deeply committed to fostering a culture of:
- **Hands-on research:** Learning by building real systems
- **Publication excellence:** Rigorous, peer-reviewed research
- **Open-source contribution:** Giving back to the community
- **Industry collaboration:** Bridging academia and practice

## GAIR Lab Vision

As the founder and director of **GAIR Lab**, Prof. Bhatia envisions a research initiative that:

1. **Conducts cutting-edge AI research** that advances both theory and practice
2. **Mentors the next generation** of AI researchers and engineers
3. **Creates open-source tools** that benefit the broader community
4. **Establishes industry partnerships** for real-world impact
5. **Publishes rigorously** in top-tier venues

## Current Research Focus

### Generative AI & Agentic Intelligence (2023 – Present)
- Agentic AI systems for cybersecurity and automation
- Multimodal intelligence for surveillance and security
- Voice synthesis and emotion-aware AI
- Scientific AI for bioinformatics and drug discovery

### Machine Learning & Deep Learning (2020 – Present)
- Applications in network security and anomaly detection
- Vision-language models and their real-world deployment
- Efficient AI for resource-constrained environments

### Network Security & Blockchain (2018 – Present)
- Smart contract security and formal verification
- IoT security architecture and threat modeling

## Academic Contributions

Prof. Bhatia has published extensively in peer-reviewed conferences and journals, with research citations across multiple domains. His work spans:

- **Network technologies:** SDN, VANET, WSN
- **IoT & ubiquitous computing:** Body area networks, wearable systems
- **Security:** Cybersecurity, blockchain, secure communications
- **Emerging AI:** Quantum computing applications, machine learning in networks
